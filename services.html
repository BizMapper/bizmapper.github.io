Directory Citation Building

Google cross-references your business information across various online sources to verify legitimacy. Consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone Number) data is vital for high local rankings.

What is included in Citation Building:

  • Submission to major directory databases (TrueLocal, Yelp, YellowPages, HotFrog, Local.com.au).
  • Manual NAP verification and clean-up of legacy incorrect records.
  • Elimination of duplicate profiles that cause search crawler confusion.
  • High-quality backlink generation from hyper-local business directories.

Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions about Local SEO or our setup process? We've got answers.

Traditional SEO focuses on ranking websites for global or national search terms. Local SEO specifically targets search intent in particular geographical areas (e.g. "plumber near me" or "best dentist in Sydney"). It relies heavily on optimizing Google My Business profiles, maps, and local directory citation structures to connect you with customers who are physically nearby and ready to buy.

Using a free public email account (like @gmail.com or @outlook.com) for business correspondence can look temporary or amateurish to new clients. Having a custom business email (like info@yourcompany.com.au) immediately establishes trust, asserts your brand identity, and indicates that your company is a registered, professional, and permanent local establishment.

While some core profile adjustments (like updating categories, operating hours, and photos) go live within a few hours, the indexing and authority build for localized search terms generally takes between 2 to 4 weeks. Consistent review collection and weekly updates help accelerate this local indexing curve.

A citation is any online mention of your business's Name, Address, and Phone Number (NAP) on third-party directory sites. Search engines utilize this NAP information to verify that your business actually exists at its registered location. If one directory lists your name as "Biz Mapper Pty Ltd" and another lists "BizMapper," Google struggles to match the profiles, reducing your local ranking authority. NAP consistency builds trust and boosts ranking potential.
